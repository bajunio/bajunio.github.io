ACHIEVEMENT THROUGH MUSIC...


...that's the bottom line. Rhythm Section is devoted to providing a creative outlet for the rhythms that drive Bay Area youth.

On the drumline our mission is to educate, develop and polish young performers in a low-stress, cooperative learning environment. In addition to musicianship, students learn the invaluable discipline, time management and teamwork skills they will need in high school and college.

STUDENTS

"Want to be a stronger drummer? Practice the following sticking patterns! Learning them will make you faster, tighter, and more masterful..."

- Mr. T

Key:

  • 'R' = Right Hand
  • 'L' = Left Hand
  • '>' = Accent (Hit Harder)

Remember to try it slowly first, then gradually speed up.
How fast can you play it without missing a beat?

Single Paradiddle

Count this: ONE-e-and-a-TWO-e-and-a-THREE-e-and-a-FOUR-e-and-a

The Flam

Count this: ONE AND TWO AND THREE AND FOUR AND

In a Flam, the "little note" is the soft hit you make with one hand right before you bring a hard hit with the other hand! However, they should be SO close together that they fit in one beat...

Double Stroke Roll

Count this: Mama-Papa-Mama-Papa-Mama-Papa-Mama-Papa

Mick Terrizzi

President, Lead Instructor

Born and raised in San Francisco, Mick Terrizzi has been putting bands together since he was in high school. During college, he toured professionally with rock and hip-hop bands throughout Southern California and Arizona. Upon acceptance to Americorps’ Teach For America program, Terrizzi traveled to Kansas City, Mo. to teach in an inner-city, low-income school district for two years. He led his students to the front page of The Kansas City Star in 2009 after forming the school’s first drumline, which toured throughout Missouri.

A passionate social studies and English classroom veteran, Terrizzi specializes in urban middle-school education. He is a graduate of San Diego State University. When not in the classroom, you can find Terrizzi behind the drums. He loves to play hip-hop and can’t say no to 90’s covers. Terrizzi’s experience as a professional teacher and musician make him an encouraging, adaptive and effective educator.

Eugene "Geno" Lucero

Vice-President, Instructor

A native of San Francisco’s Mission District, Geno Lucero brings 40 years of professional music experience to Rhythm Section Drumlines. A talented drummer, Lucero also specializes in music equipment, professional stage sound and lighting. He began touring with the Rolling Stones in 1975 as keyboard technician to Billy Preston. He returned to San Francisco briefly before joining Carlos Santana as a member of his touring team. Geno has enjoyed a passion for the arts throughout his life.

When not volunteering his time to refurbish donated drums or assist students with a cadence, Lucero continues his work as a professional stagehand in San Francisco. You can find him on the stages of Outside Lands Music and Arts Festival, behind the scenes at the Moscone Center or manning a spotlight when Sir Paul McCartney is in town.

Nicole Terrizzi

Board Chair, Grant Writer

Nicole Terrizzi grew up with a passion for music and language, and has sought to share that passion throug h teaching and grant writing. Originally from a small farming community in Iowa, she played the snare drum in her school drumline before going on to study journalism and classical opera at Simpson College. She was selected for Americorps’ Teach for America program and served as part of the Kansas City charter corps. Terrizzi taught second grade and middle school while earning her Masters in Secondary Education from the University of Missouri St. Louis. Upon graduation, she moved to California where she taught middle school English and history in Oakland.

Terrizzi has since worked in marketing, fundraising and proposal writing for education organizations, and currently works as a grant writer at a small non-profit that advocates for small business owners.
