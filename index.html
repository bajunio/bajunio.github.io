ACHIEVEMENT THROUGH MUSIC...


...that's the bottom line. Rhythm Section is devoted to providing a creative outlet for the rhythms that drive Bay Area youth.

On the drumline our mission is to educate, develop and polish young performers in a low-stress, cooperative learning environment. In addition to musicianship, students learn the invaluable discipline, time management and teamwork skills they will need in high school and college.

STUDENTS

"Want to be a stronger drummer? Practice the following sticking patterns! Learning them will make you faster, tighter, and more masterful..."

- Mr. T

Key:

  • 'R' = Right Hand
  • 'L' = Left Hand
  • '>' = Accent (Hit Harder)

Remember to try it slowly first, then gradually speed up.
How fast can you play it without missing a beat?

Single Paradiddle

Count this: ONE-e-and-a-TWO-e-and-a-THREE-e-and-a-FOUR-e-and-a

The Flam

Count this: ONE AND TWO AND THREE AND FOUR AND

In a Flam, the "little note" is the soft hit you make with one hand right before you bring a hard hit with the other hand! However, they should be SO close together that they fit in one beat...

Double Stroke Roll

Count this: Mama-Papa-Mama-Papa-Mama-Papa-Mama-Papa

Mick Terrizzi

President, Lead Instructor

Born and raised in San Francisco, Mick has been putting bands together since he was in high school. During college, Terrizzi toured professionally with rock and hip-hop bands throughout Southern California and Arizona. Upon acceptance to Americorps' Teach For America program, Terrizzi traveled to Kansas City, MO. to teach in an inner city, low-income school district for two years. He lead his students to the front page of the city's newspaper in 2009 after forming the school's first drumline, which toured throughout Missouri.

A passionate Social Studies and English classroom veteran, Terrizzi specializes in middle school and junior high education. When not in the classroom, you can find Mick behind the drums. He loves to play hip-hop, and can't say no to 90s covers.

Mick is a graduate of San Diego State University. Specialized in urban education, his experience in an array of classrooms makes him an encouraging, adaptable, effective educator in both music and academia. He currently teaches jr. high humanities in the Mission District of San Francisco.

Eugene "Geno" Lucero

Vice-President, Instructor

A native to San Francisco's Mission District, Geno brings 40 years of professional music experience to Rhythm Section. A talented drummer, Lucero also specializes in equipment and professional stage sound and lighting. After touring with The Rolling Stones in 1975 as keyboard technician to Billy Preston, he returned to San Francisco briefly before joining Carlos Santana as a member of his touring team. Geno has enjoyed a passion for the arts ever since.

When not volunteering his time to refurbish donated drums or assist students with a cadence, Geno continues his work as a professional stagehand in San Francisco. You can find him on the stages of the Outside Lands Festival, behind the scenes at the Moscone Center, or manning a spotlight when Sir. Paul McCartney is in town.

Nicole Terrizzi

Board Chair, Grant Writer

Nicole enjoys a passion for music and language. Hailing from Northwest Iowa, she studied journalism and classical opera at Simpson College before being selected for Americorps' Teach For America program and receiving her two year assignment to teach in Kansas City, MO. Upon earning her Masters in Secondary Education from University of Missouri-St. Louis, Cleveringa moved to California to teach jr. high literature and social studies in Oakland for two more years. She led her students to significant academic gains.

Presently, Nicole writes for an education company in San Francisco that designs teacher assessment tools. She is the director of Reading Partners within her company, a volunteer tutoring program that gives students in San Francisco's Tenderloin one-on-one assistance with literacy.
